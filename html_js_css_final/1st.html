Lionel Messi, full name Lionel Andrés Messi, also known as Leo Messi, (born June 24, 1987 in Rosario, Argentina), Argentinian football (soccer) player who received a record seven Balls Gold as the best player in the world men's tennis player (2009-12, 2015, 2019 and 2021). In 2022, he helped Argentina win the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) World Football Championship.

Early life


Messi started playing football as a boy and in 1995 joined the youth team of Newell's Old Boys (a first-class football club based in Rosario). Messi's extraordinary skills have caught the eye of prestigious clubs on both sides of the Atlantic. At the age of 13, Messi and his family moved to Barcelona and he started playing for FC Barcelona's U14 team. He scored 21 goals in 14 games for the youth team, and he quickly rose through the higher ranks until, at the age of 16, he made his official debut for FC Barcelona in a friendly match.

club game

In the 2004-05 season, the then 17-year-old Messi became the youngest official player and scorer in Spain's La Liga (the country's top football league). Although he is only 1.7 meters (5 ft 7 in) tall and weighs 67 kg (148 lbs), he is strong, well-proportioned and versatile on the pitch.
Naturally left-footed, with quick and precise ball control, Messi is a keen pass distributor and can easily break through a crowded defence. In 2005, he was granted Spanish citizenship, an honor greeted with mixed emotions by Barcelona's passionate Catalan fans. The following year, Messi and Barcelona won the Champions League (European Club Championship).


Messi's form continued to improve rapidly over the years and in 2008 he was one of the most dominant players in the world, coming in second to Manchester United's Cristiano Ronaldo in the Ballon d'Or poll. a spectacular 2008-09 season by helping FC Barcelona win the club's first 'Triple' (winning three major European club titles in a single season): The team won La Liga, the Copa del Rey (Spain's biggest national cup) and the Champions League title. He scored 38 goals in 51 appearances that season, and he beat Ronaldo in the FIFA Ballon d'Or and FIFA World Player of the Year by a record margin. During the 2009-10 season, Messi scored 34 goals in domestic matches to help Barcelona repeat the La Liga title. He won the Golden Shoe award as Europe's top scorer and he received another Ballon d'Or (award known as the 2010-15 FIFA Golden Ball).
